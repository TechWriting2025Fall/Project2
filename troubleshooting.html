Troubleshooting Tips

subheading

Print won't stick to the bed

  • The nozzle may be too far from the bed. Ensure the gap is correct when you trying to print.
  • Oils from fingers or dust can prevent adhesion. Clean the surface thoroughly with Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA) or soap and water
  • Use Adhesion Aids- Apply a thin layer of glue stick, hairspray, or painter's tape to the print surface.

First layer is too squished

  • The nozzle is too close to the bed, blocking the filament flow. Raise the Z-offset slightly.
  • Ensure the bed is perfectly level across all points.

edges of the print curl up off the print bed during the print

  • Add a raft in your slicer to increase the surface area touching the bed.
  • Use an enclosure to maintain a stable, warm temperature around the print, especially for materials like ABS.
  • Eliminate cold air currents that can cause uneven cooling and shrinking.
